I’m a happy generalist who does a lot of designing through code. With a background ranging from print design to user experience to front-end development, I now spend most of my time building digital publications and online editorial experiences. Whether my medium is a printed page or a smartphone, I aim to design things that people will love to read.

I currently work with the fine folks at Upstatement in Boston. I’m the Design Lead of Release Team, which focuses on the long-term growth and evolution of websites. We’re essentially remote product design for organizations without in-house digital teams. I’ve been lucky to work with a wide range of clients — sometimes for months, sometimes for years — including The Trace, WBUR, Christopher Kimball’s Milk Street, The Engine, Taste, and Punch.

If you’d like to chat about typography, modular Sass structure, bocce technique, weird amaros, or the finer points of homemade pierogi, don’t hesitate to say hi via email or Twitter. I’m from the Midwest, so I’m more or less culturally predisposed to chattiness.
